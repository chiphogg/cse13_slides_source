First try: pointwise uncertainty

One common approach is to give some kind of credible interval at each point. These usually won't vary much from point to point, so they come out looking pretty smooth. (Here's a 1D example for visual clarity.)

The problem is, you have thrown away any information about how the points relate to each other. This information is critically important; for instance, in this case, actual members of the distribution look more like this, or this; the credible interval approach can mislead.

Not to mention, in 2D, it can be kind of hard to see the credible intervals.
